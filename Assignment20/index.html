Why is the sky blue but clouds are white?

Answer

The sky is blue because the molecules in the air scatter light in the higher wavelengths (that is, the blue light), while lower wavelength light (that is, the light on the red end of the spectrum) goes through to the ground. So the light that get finally reflected down from the scattering is blue, therefore a blue sky. This is called Rayleigh scattering. Clouds are usually white due to the reflection of light off the water vapor and the ice crystals in the clouds.
